This dashboard shows information about company employees and who is leaving the company.
It helps HR understand:

How many people work in the company
How many people left (attrition)
From which department people are leaving more
Which age group and gender are leaving more
How happy employees are with their jobs

So, it is mainly used to reduce employee turnover and improve employee satisfaction

Main Numbers at the Bottom

These are quick summary numbers:

Total Employees: 1470 people in company
Active Employees: 1233 still working
 Attrition: 237 people left the job
Attrition Rate: 16.1% of employees left
Average Age: 37 years

This tells HR how healthy the company workforce is.

Which Department Has More People Leaving?

Shown in the round (pie) chart:

R&D – most people left
Sales – second highest
HR – very few people left

This helps HR focus more on departments where problems are high.

Employees by Age and Gender

The bar chart shows:

Age groups like: Under 25, 25–34, 35–44, etc.
Blue and dark color shows male and female

We can see:

Most employees are between 25 to 44 years
We can compare how many men and women are in each age group

This helps in understanding workforce balance

From Which Education Field People Are Leaving More

This chart shows people who left from different education backgrounds:

Life Science – most people left

Medical – second

Marketing and Technical – medium

HR – very few

This helps HR understand which type of employees need more support.
 How Happy Employees Are With Their Jobs

The table shows:

Different job roles like Manager, Sales Executive, Scientist, etc.

Ratings from 1 (not happy) to 4 (very happy)

 HR can see which job roles are less happy and may leave in future.
 Who Is Leaving by Age and Gender

Small round charts show:

For each age group, how many men and women left
Example: In 25–34 age group, more people are leaving compared to others

 This helps HR target specific groups for retention.
Filter Buttons (Top Right)

Buttons like:
